Walking With Impact

In reflecting about who I am becoming and where I am going, it often comes into my awareness about the impact that someone might have upon my life. It's easy to take for granted sometimes that one little choice we make can have significant effects that ripple out on our lives and perhaps the world. For example, choosing to make a new relationship, going to a new event that may not be an event you may not choose to go to normally, making new connections. Or sometimes it's just saying the right thing at the right time to cause a cascade of events that shape someone's life.

I've certainly had a number of those life changing simple choices. I've met many people who just off a whim I say yes to do something, and my whole life is different because I made that choice.

Yesterday I met up with someone I hadn't seen in 8 years. It was this morning I had realized the impact she made on my life the last time we chatted.

We both went to high school together, and during the later years, say junior year, is when we met. We both smoked a lot of pot with a not-so inspiring group of people, skipping school, getting into trouble for smoking, and just being rebellious were the activities we both chose. Thankfully, I don't recall either one of us getting arrested.

Change happened during our senior year, and I think she moved away, so she drifted from my awareness. At 21 I started to get into some really hard drugs, and did a lot of them. About that time she showed up randomly, visiting her father in my home town. Some of the conversations we had we not so pleasant for me because I was getting into crystal meth at that time, knowing that where I was at in my life was soul-crushing. In a way, she sat there with me, in compassion, hoping I would see the goodness in my heart to start changing my life; in a way she was calling to me to get out of that small town.

We drifted apart again shortly after, mostly through my own doing, and had a series of events that at 22 I decided to get out Arlington, my home town, and into Seattle. Hands down the best choice of my life.

Meeting with her last night revealed to me so much about what transpired those days 8 years ago. The reflection of our encounter revealed just how much I have grown, despite my daily struggles within myself. I felt empowered, strong, courageous, and confident that my choices had really helped bring me to today. And all it took was compassion for a hurt little boy those fateful days.

This is but one person who has walked this journey with me at some point, and so much gratitude is felt in my heart for these people. Some are fortunate enough to make a choice for their own empowerment, and others choose to stay within the confines of their personal safety, regardless on how their choices affect them. Either way, we all have people walking by our side for any length of period, as we walk by theirs. Never take for granted those little choices we make, nor those people who walk by our side.
