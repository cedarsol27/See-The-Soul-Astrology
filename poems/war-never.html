Poetry

The War That Never Was

All my heart is protected by chains
All I could ever feel, just outside the door
All the blessings, I don't want to know
All the love in misery, I don't want to feel
Confused as to why I chained this heart
Carrying around the burden of dead weight
The punishment of my stories my stubbornness persists
A war long past, I carry my weapon
To defend upon any aggressor, my self the worst
To wake and aware, the danger of something sealed
So precious, so real, so meaningful
But don't come near as I fear this cannot be undone
Why throw myself into the flames of love
When being away has provided such comfort
I care not of illness, because the imagery is so alluring
I care not of beauty, because despair is lustful
And I am a man of instant gratification
This permanent guarantee is my only validation
Do not whisper in my ear that you are here, my Love
As I am committed to living a life of war
Even as you wait, relentlessly
For now I must disagree
But the only words that ever penetrate
Is that you are always here
When I am ready
When I tire
When I surrender
And then, I will realize my chains were never real
And I will be that tenderness and softness
I have always been
And to know that this war I waged
Never was there
